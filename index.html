Who Resides in NYC Homeless Shelters?

By Rizwana Zafer
Dec. 17, 2021

According to data provided by the Department of Homeless Services, Brooklyn community district 5 and Bronx community district 1 have the highest number of shelter residents in New York City. The number of individuals experiencing homelessness has only been increasing since the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Those experiencing homelessness are often from underserved communities and BIPOC backgrounds.

Kevin Li, an attorney for CAMBA Legal Services in South Brooklyn, works with individuals who are experiencing housing issues, including eviction and homelessness.

“Poverty in New York City is a generational issue that often revolves around race, with minorities often more likely than not to be impoverished,” says Li. “Almost all of our clients are minorities facing tough financial circumstances and are facing nonpayment cases, which makes it harder to ‘win’ and avoid eviction.”

New shelters are continuing to be built across the city to combat increasing post-pandemic levels of homelessness.
